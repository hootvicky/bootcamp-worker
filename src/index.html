🃏 Network Architecture Cards

Flip each card to learn what makes Cloudflare's network unique. Flip all 5 to complete this activity.

🏗️
Every Service in Every Server
Tap to flip
The "Every Server" Architecture
Unlike scrubbing centers that only run DDoS mitigation in specific locations, every Cloudflare service — WAF, CDN, DDoS, Zero Trust — runs on every server in every data center. Network capacity = mitigation capacity = 477 Tbps.
📡
Anycast Routing
Tap to flip
Anycast: Closest Server Wins
Cloudflare uses Anycast networking — all data centers share the same IP address. When a user (or attacker) sends traffic, it automatically routes to the nearest Cloudflare location. Attack traffic gets absorbed closest to its source, before it can overwhelm a single point.
💣
DDoS: What It Is
Tap to flip
Distributed Denial of Service
A DDoS attack floods your infrastructure with fake traffic to make it unavailable to real users. It's low-cost for attackers, high-cost for victims. In 2025 alone, Cloudflare mitigated 47 million DDoS attacks — 73% more than 2024. 89% last under 10 minutes ("hit and run").
🚂
Magic Transit
Tap to flip
Magic Transit: L3 DDoS Protection
Magic Transit protects entire IP address ranges (prefixes). Traffic is ingested via BGP route announcements, inspected at every Cloudflare PoP, then clean traffic is returned via GRE/IPsec tunnels. Always-On mode detects and mitigates in ~3 seconds — before on-demand tools even activate.
🧹
Why Scrubbing Centers Fail
Tap to flip
The Scrubbing Center Problem
Legacy DDoS solutions use centralized scrubbing centers — dedicated locations that clean traffic. Problems: (1) They add latency by re-routing traffic. (2) They're capacity-limited — competitors like Akamai Prolexic have 15 Tbps; the 31.4 Tbps record attack would have taken them offline. Cloudflare's 477 Tbps distributed network handled it with headroom to spare.

🗂️ Deep Dive: The Three Pillars

Click each card to explore each phase within the three pillars

🛡️ Pillar 1 — Application Services
📖

DNS & CDN

Click to reveal

DNS is the "phonebook of the internet." Cloudflare's CDN caches content globally so a user in Tokyo loads from a nearby data center, not a server in London. Speed = revenue.

🌊

DDoS & Magic Transit

Click to reveal

DDoS floods a site with fake traffic to crash it. Cloudflare absorbs the attack at the network edge. Magic Transit extends this to entire IP ranges — protecting a company's whole network, not just one website.

🤖

WAF, API Security & Bots

Click to reveal

WAF blocks the OWASP Top 10 (SQL injection, XSS). API Security schema-validates requests — APIs are hackers' new favourite target. Bot Management separates good bots (Google) from bad bots (scrapers, scalpers).

🔐 Pillar 2 — SASE & Networking
💀

Death of the VPN

Click to reveal

Traditional VPNs are slow, over-privileged, and hated. Zero Trust replaces them: "Never Trust, Always Verify." Every request is checked for identity and device health before granting access — even inside the network.

🏢

Next-Gen Firewall

Click to reveal

Legacy on-prem firewalls are expensive hardware boxes. Cloudflare moves the firewall to the software edge — no hardware to maintain, instant global policy updates, and always-on inspection.

🗺️

SASE vs. SSE

Click to reveal

SSE = Security Service Edge (just the security tools). SASE = SSE + Networking (SD-WAN). Cloudflare is a SASE leader because we own the global network. Competitors bolt on networking — we built it in from day one.

🛠️ Pillar 3 — Developer Platform
⚡

Cloudflare Workers

Click to reveal

"Serverless" doesn't mean no servers — it means the developer doesn't manage them. Workers runs JavaScript/TypeScript code in milliseconds at the edge, closest to the user. No cold starts. No ops headache.

🗄️

R2, D1 & KV

Click to reveal

R2 = object storage with zero egress fees (vs. AWS S3's painful bills). D1 = SQL database at the edge. KV = ultra-fast key-value store. Full-stack apps built entirely on Cloudflare — no AWS, no Azure needed.

🧠

AI on the Edge

Click to reveal

Workers AI lets developers run AI inference (e.g. Llama, Mistral) directly on Cloudflare's global network — no expensive GPU instances required. AI Gateway manages, observes, and caches AI traffic across any provider.

👥 The 2026 Roster: Who Is Who

Flip each card to learn what each role owns. You need to know these to build your deal team. Flip all 8 to complete this activity.

🧑‍💼

Account Executive (AE)

Click to reveal

The "CEO of the business." Leads the full sales process end-to-end — from Qualify/Discovery through to Close. Owns the customer relationship and coordinates the entire deal team.

💻

Inside Sales Rep (ISR)

Click to reveal

A new 2026 role for virtual selling in the Commercial segment — accounts with <150 employees. 10 ISRs + 1 ISR Manager launched across APAC. Cost-effective motion for smaller accounts.

🔧

SE / SA

Click to reveal

Solution Engineer (SE) and Solution Architect (SA) are the technical owners. SEs lead Discovery (technical validation) and Solution Design (architecture definition). PSG specialists join for Zero Trust / CF1 opportunities.

📣

BDR

Click to reveal

Business Development Rep. Handles inbound and outbound lead generation — all pre-pipeline activity. Passes qualified leads to the AE. Does NOT own the sales process beyond handoff.

🏦

Deal Desk (DD)

Click to reveal

Centralized team managing complex or non-standard deals. Engaged when discounting exceeds 80% (deal moves from Sales Manager approval to Deal Desk). Maximizes revenue and minimizes commercial risk.

🤝

PAM / IPAM

Click to reveal

Partner Account Manager (PAM) manages external focus partners. IPAM manages internal partner alignment. Per 2026 strategy, all deals should be partner-attached. Partners must register deals (PIO) to earn highest discounts and deal protection. Target: 90%+ partner attach by 2028.

⚙️

Sales Ops & Legal

Click to reveal

Sales Operations handles territory carving, forecasting, and GTM planning. Legal & Contracts manages redlines and ensures Enterprise Subscription Agreement (ESA) compliance. Both are engaged at the Negotiate Proposal phase.

🎯

PSG & Marketing

Click to reveal

Product Specialization & Growth (PSG) focuses on high-growth areas like Zero Trust / CF1. For Cloudflare One deals (Magic WAN / Zero Trust), always engage a Partner Service Delivery Manager (PSDM) to find an Authorized Service Delivery Partner (ASDP). Marketing / ABM warms strategic accounts with events and targeted campaigns.
